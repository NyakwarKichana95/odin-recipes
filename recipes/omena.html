Omena Recipe

Description

Omena is a small fresh water fish that resembles sardines. The name Omena is actually derived from the Luo Language and simply refers to the Silver Cyprin fish in English.

It is a popular dish in Kenya Kenya, Tanzania, as well as other parts of Africa, where it goes by several names such as Lake victoria Sardine, Dagaa (Tanzania), or Mukene(Uganda). It is normally prepared into stew, shallow fried or deep fried.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of Omena
  • 3 cups of water
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil
  • 3 tomatoes
  • 1 large Onion
  • Garlic (don't be shy with them)
  • 1 cup heavy cream / coconut milk
  • Salt

Directions

  1. Wash the Omena in cold water.
  2. Put the Omena in a pan and let it simmer gently in 3 cups of water until its almost gone
  3. peal your tomatoes by dipping them in boiling water
  4. Dice you onions and tomatoes
  5. In a frying pan heat oil
  6. Add your onions and let them cook to your liking
  7. Add tomatoes and stir adding some salk
  8. Throw in a generous amount of garlic stir and let ir cook for a min or two
  9. Add the Omena into the mix and stir
  10. Let it cook for a couple mins. Feel free to add spices of your liking
  11. Add your heavy cream or coconut milk and let it simmer on medium-low heat for 10-15 mins.
  12. Serve with Ugali
