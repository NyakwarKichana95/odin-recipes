Aliya Stew Recipe

Description

Aliya is smoked and sun dried meat. So basically raw meat is sliced into thin pieces then it is sun dried during the day and smoked during the night. this is a common practice within the Luo tribe in Kenay.

The is done to preserve the meat for later use since there are no fridges and freezers in the village. The minimum number of days to dry and smoke the meat to make sure it does not go bad is three days

After the three days you can choose to continue smoking it or you can just save it for later date or eat it your choice

P.S this dish is way more delicions if cooked in Agulu (clay pot)

Ingredients

  • Dried and smoked beef
  • Cooking oil
  • Water
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Cilantro
  • Spices of your choosing (Don't be the mumu who does not flavor their food)

Steps

  1. Cut your beef into pieces
  2. Put the beef in a sufuria and add water
  3. Boil the beef for at least and hour to tenderise it (this will also be its shower)
  4. Dice you onions and tomatoes
  5. In a pot add oil then onions ond cook to your desired liking
  6. Add tomoatoes and garlic, cook as you stir until its a nice looking yummy paste
  7. Into the pot add your already boiled dried beef with all the juices
  8. Mix well as you add you spices for taste and if you need more soup add a cup or more of water/milk
  9. Let it simmer on medium-low heat for 20-30 mins
  10. Top it with your Cilantro
  11. Serve with Ugali and herbs of your choosing
